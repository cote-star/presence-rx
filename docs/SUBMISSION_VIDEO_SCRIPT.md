# 2-Minute Submission Video Script

## Narrative Principle

Lead with the user outcome, not the technology.

The video should feel like a marketer opening the product and immediately understanding:

1. What is wrong with the brand's AI presence.
2. What to do next.
3. What not to claim.
4. Why the system is trustworthy.
5. How this extends Peec AI into an AI-marketer workflow.

## Timing

- **0:00-1:30** — Webapp demo from user perspective
- **1:30-2:00** — Architecture and partner-tech explanation

## Script

### 0:00-0:12 — Open With The Result

**Screen:** Lovable / webapp overview page, Nothing Phone selected.

**Voiceover:**

Presence Rx starts where a marketer starts: what should I do next? For Nothing Phone, the answer is clear. Peec shows the brand ranks well when it appears, but it is missing from most AI answers. So this is not a quality problem. It is a presence problem.

**Show:**

- Overall visibility
- Position / rank when mentioned
- Strategic gaps or action cards

### 0:12-0:30 — Explain The Core Insight

**Screen:** Overview action cards or Diagnosis page.

**Voiceover:**

The product does not just say "visibility is low." It tells the marketer what kind of problem each gap is. Smartphone Design is a strength to defend. Minimalist Hardware is a strategic perception gap: Nothing's brand is built around minimalism, but AI associates that language with Apple. Wireless Audio is different. Nothing has real products there, but the public signal is too thin.

**Show:**

- Smartphone Design as strength
- Minimalist Hardware as strategic gap
- Wireless Audio as attention / volume gap
- Status colors and recommended actions

### 0:30-0:48 — Show The AI Marketer Capability

**Screen:** Diagnosis or Analytics page with priority signals.

**Voiceover:**

This is the AI marketer layer on top of Peec. We combine Peec visibility with strategic intent: does the brand want this association, how important is it, and what ambition level is realistic? That prevents bad recommendations, like telling Nothing to fight Apple for "mobile ecosystem leader" when the better claim is a stylish, cohesive product family for a specific audience.

**Show:**

- Strategic status
- Ambition / positioning frame if visible
- Action Priority
- Non-priority or monitor topic if available

### 0:48-1:08 — Show Evidence And Transparency

**Screen:** Evidence page.

**Voiceover:**

Every recommendation has receipts. Peec is the source of truth for AI visibility. Tavily adds public web proof and editorial gaps. Gemini identifies perception themes and missing associations. The evidence ledger keeps those sources separate, so we never pretend a generated insight is the same as a measured visibility signal.

**Show:**

- Evidence ledger summary
- Claims / evidence table
- Source or method labels

### 1:08-1:28 — The Claim Guardrail Demo

**Screen:** Claim simulator.

**Voiceover:**

The most important feature is that the system can say no. If I test a tempting claim like "Nothing Phone is the go-to minimalist tech brand," Presence Rx blocks it. The reason is explainable: Nothing has only low AI visibility for Minimalist Hardware while Apple owns the association. Then it gives the safer claim: Nothing has a distinctive take on minimalism, but there is still a gap between brand intent and AI perception.

**Show:**

- Enter or select the blocked claim
- Blocked result
- Reason
- Safe rewrite
- Brand positioning frame if available

### 1:28-1:30 — Transition

**Screen:** Keep simulator result visible, then cut to architecture diagram.

**Voiceover:**

Here is how it works.

### 1:30-1:52 — Architecture Diagram

**Screen:** `docs/submission-architecture.html` or exported diagram.

**Voiceover:**

Presence Rx starts with brand strategy and audience profiles: what the brand wants to mean, and who it wants to matter to. That becomes a prompt universe. Peec MCP measures how the brand actually appears in AI answers. Tavily adds public proof. Gemini analyzes perception themes. Then Presence Rx scores the evidence, classifies the gap type, sets action priority, and checks whether the marketing claim is earned.

**Show:**

- Inputs lane
- Signals lane
- Presence Rx Intelligence lane

### 1:52-2:00 — Close On Improvement Over Peec

**Screen:** Architecture output lane or dashboard export page.

**Voiceover:**

So the improvement on top of Peec is the decision layer: not just where am I visible, but what should I do, what can I safely say, what should I monitor next, and what claims should I refuse. The output is a Lovable dashboard, an evidence ledger, an action brief, and Peec monitoring prompts.

**Show:**

- Lovable dashboard
- Verdict Pack
- Peec Writes / monitoring prompts

## Ultra-Tight Backup Script

Use this if the demo runs long.

Presence Rx turns Peec visibility data into an AI-marketer workflow. For Nothing Phone, Peec shows a sharp story: the brand performs well when mentioned, but it is absent from most AI answers. Presence Rx explains why. It separates strengths from strategic gaps, classifies whether each gap is perception, discovery, or attention, and ties every recommendation to evidence.

Here, Minimalist Hardware is the key issue. Nothing is built around minimalist design, but AI associates that language with Apple. The system then checks claims. "Nothing Phone is the go-to minimalist tech brand" is blocked because the evidence does not support category ownership. Instead, the system gives a safer rewrite and the next evidence to build.

Architecturally, Peec is the source of truth for AI visibility. Tavily adds public proof, Gemini identifies perception themes, and Presence Rx adds the decision layer: strategic intent, audience fit, gap type, evidence scoring, and claim guardrails. The result is a Lovable dashboard, an evidence ledger, an action brief, and monitoring prompts back into Peec.

## Phrases To Use

- "Peec measures the AI answer reality."
- "Presence Rx turns that measurement into marketing decisions."
- "The no is the feature."
- "Every recommendation has receipts."
- "We separate measured visibility from interpreted perception."
- "This is not a generic content generator. It is an evidence-graded AI marketer."

## Phrases To Avoid

- "Gemini measures visibility."
- "Tavily proves the truth."
- "The system guarantees what marketers should say."
- "Nothing is the leading ecosystem brand."
- "The AI wrote the strategy."
