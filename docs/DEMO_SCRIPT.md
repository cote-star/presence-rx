# Presence Rx — 2-Minute Demo Script

**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover. 90s webapp demo + 30s architecture.
**Principle:** Result first. User perspective. Every claim has receipts.

---

## 0:00–0:10 — Open With The Problem (Nothing Phone)

**Screen:** Webapp landing page, Nothing Phone selected. KPI cards visible.

**Voice:**
"Nothing Phone ranks near the top when AI mentions it — but most AI answers don't mention it at all. Peec shows the data. Presence Rx tells the marketer what to do about it."

**Show:** KPI cards (avg visibility, position, confirmed gaps, claims to avoid, action priority).

---

## 0:10–0:30 — The Strategic Layer (Action Brief)

**Screen:** Scroll through action cards. Show strategic status badges.

**Voice:**
"Not every gap is the same problem. Smartphone Design is an owned strength — defend it. Minimalist Hardware is the urgent one: Nothing's brand IS minimalism, but AI associates that word with Apple. That's a perception gap. Wireless Audio is different — Nothing Ear exists, but public coverage is too thin. That's an attention gap. And Mobile Ecosystem is a strategic gap with niche ambition: Nothing should claim a cohesive product family, not Apple-style ecosystem leadership."

**Show:**
- Owned Strength (green) on Smartphone Design
- Strategic Gap (red) on Minimalist Hardware — core importance
- Attention gap on Wireless Audio
- Strategic Gap on Mobile Ecosystem, but with niche-alternative ambition
- Gap types: Perception / Discovery / Attention alongside strategic status

---

## 0:30–0:45 — Conversation Blocks + Perception

**Screen:** Click to Diagnosis page (Conversation Blocks).

**Voice:**
"Each topic gets a full conversation block. Here's Minimalist Hardware: 6% visibility, Apple owns it at 39%. Gemini's perception analysis shows AI uses adjacent words like 'stylish' for Nothing but reserves 'minimalist' for Apple. The strategic context says this is core identity — the most urgent gap to close."

**Show:**
- Blind spot card with strategic badge + context box
- Perception themes from Gemini
- Recommended next move
- Strategic note: "CORE IDENTITY"

---

## 0:45–1:00 — The Claim Guardrail (Simulator)

**Screen:** Click to Evidence page. Scroll to Claim Simulator.

**Voice:**
"The most important feature is that the system says no. Watch: 'Nothing Phone is the go-to minimalist tech brand.' Blocked. The reason: 6% visibility while Apple holds 39%. Ownership claims exceed the allowed ceiling. Here's the safe rewrite — and notice it uses the brand's own positioning language, not a generic template."

**Show:**
- Type or click the tempting claim
- Red "Blocked" badge appears
- Reason with percentages
- Brand-voice safe rewrite
- Try a second claim on the stronghold topic → green "Safe"

---

## 1:00–1:10 — Evidence Receipts

**Screen:** Scroll up to Evidence Summary + Claims table.

**Voice:**
"Every recommendation traces back to evidence. Peec is the source of truth for visibility. Tavily adds public web proof. Gemini identifies perception themes. The ledger keeps them separate — we never pretend a generated insight is a measured signal. Non-priority topics show as 'monitor only,' not publishable."

**Show:**
- Evidence summary tiles (claims, methods, sources)
- Claims table with status pills (directional green, blocked red, monitor-only gray)

---

## 1:10–1:20 — Brand Switch (Generalizability)

**Screen:** Click brand tab: Attio.

**Voice:**
"This isn't a one-brand demo. Switch to Attio — a SaaS CRM. Same framework, different story. Product-Led CRM is their strength. CRM for Startups is a strategic gap against HubSpot. CRM Migration is non-priority — they're not fighting that war."

**Show:**
- Brand tabs switch (Nothing Phone → Attio)
- Different KPIs, different gaps, different strategic statuses
- CRM Migration marked as non-priority / monitor

---

## 1:20–1:30 — Analytics Flash

**Screen:** Click Analytics. Optional one-second flash of Future page only if timing is smooth.

**Voice:**
"The analytics show visibility by strategic status, gap type mix, competitor landscape, and a signal profile per topic. This is where the marketer can see whether the problem is positioning, discovery, or attention — and which competitor owns the narrative."

**Show:**
- Visibility bars colored by strategic status (2 seconds)
- Gap Mix donut (1 second)
- Competitor landscape (1 second)
- Optional: Future page with amber "illustrative" banner (only if time allows)

---

## 1:30–1:52 — Architecture (Diagram)

**Screen:** Architecture diagram from docs/.

**Voice:**
"Presence Rx starts with brand strategy — what the brand wants to mean and who it matters to. Peec MCP measures how the brand actually appears in AI answers. Tavily adds public web evidence. Gemini analyzes perception themes. Then Presence Rx adds the decision layer: strategic intent, gap classification, evidence scoring, claim ceilings, and action priority. Every output is evidence-gated."

**Show:** Architecture diagram with lanes:
- Inputs: brand strategy, audience, prompts
- Signals: Peec MCP, Tavily, Gemini
- Intelligence: gap classifier, claim check, action priority
- Outputs: dashboard, verdict, action brief, Peec monitoring prompts

---

## 1:52–2:00 — Close

**Screen:** Back to webapp, Nothing Phone action brief visible.

**Voice:**
"The improvement on top of Peec: not just where am I visible, but what should I do, what can I safely claim, what should I refuse, and what should I monitor next. Presence Rx — diagnose, prescribe, refuse."

**Show:** Logo / tagline card.

---

## Key Phrases

- "Peec measures the AI answer reality."
- "Presence Rx turns that measurement into marketing decisions."
- "The no is the feature."
- "Every recommendation has receipts."
- "We separate measured visibility from interpreted perception."
- "This is not a content generator. It's an evidence-graded AI marketer."

## Avoid

- "Gemini measures visibility." (It doesn't — Peec does.)
- "The AI wrote the strategy." (Strategy is human + data, not generated.)
- "Nothing is the leading ecosystem brand." (Off-strategy claim.)
